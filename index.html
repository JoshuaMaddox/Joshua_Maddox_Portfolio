Joshua Maddox

I am passionate about using marketing, code, design and entrepreneurship as tools for social good and poverty elimination. I've often said I would've loved to have been a full-time student; luckily, I've chosen a cereer that requires an insatiable appetite for learning...or else.

'07 — Current Cinderhouse

What I Do

Cinderhouse is an ongoing pet project that began as a creative outlet and testing ground for marketing and design concepts but turned into a successful social enterprise. I am a co-founder, active designer / marketer and floor sweeper.

Why it's Relevant

Cinderhouse allows us to imagine, test, fail and succeed at the concepts we're normally not allowed to play with while in more civilized companies. All of the experimenting and learning we do benefits the companies we work for both from the knowledge we gain and in that we expel some of our least controlled creative urges. Cinderhouse uses profit from the work we do to test, found and fund social enterprise structures that use 100% of profit to battle poverty and inequality.

'09 — '15 British School Nanjing

What I Did

As Head of Administration, I was directly responsible for a staff of 19, marketing and all non-academic management including campus oversight and contract negotiations for one of the most esteemed International schools in Nanjing, China.

Why it's Relevant

I wrote and implemented creative marketing strategies that grew revenue by 350%, successfully oversaw a new 15,000 SQM campus building project, restructured administration management to save 125K+ USD annually while improving efficacy AND once, during land contract negotiations with top level city officials, ate live shrimp soaked in wine without retching(mainly). The lessons I learned while at BSN were far greater than just knowing that one should start diner meetings with a lie about being allergic to shellfish.

'08 — '09 Hongren

What I Did

I was hired as the Director of Business Development and was responsible for designing and implementing marketing and general business development initiatives in the support of sales. I was promoted to head of the campus program shortly after starting.

Why it's Relevant

I was responsible for a staff of 24, a 12,000 SQM+ campus and over 400 live-in students. As Chinese was the main language used for management, I had to accelerate my Mandarin studies and subject myself to good hearted but well deserved ridicule of my language and cultural inadequacies. Although, I learned a lot about day to day management, marketing the program, working with clients etc...the most valuable lessons came in learning how to work with staff of an array of nationationalities and cultures while equitably navigating the conflicts and misunderstandings that arose.

'06 — '08 JBM

What I Did

Founded a soft-skills consultancy in Beijing during the lead-up to the 2008 Olympics. We managed client accounts around Beijing from the Central Business District to Shangdi(Beijing's Silicon Valley). I became well acquainted with the 48 hour work day.

Why It's Relevant

We advised companies such as IBM-Lenovo, LG, Langham Hotels, Elong.com, Match.com etc... in soft-skills management and business communication towards their overseas counterparts and clients. Because our services had to be tailored according to individual department needs, I was allowed to peer directly into and learn from an array of international management structures. I also learned what it means to fight for life as a small start-up in a saturated market via creative marketing and winning contracts by converting client problems into concierge service offerings.
